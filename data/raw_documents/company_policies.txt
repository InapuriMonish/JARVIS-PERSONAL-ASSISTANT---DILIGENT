COMPANY POLICIES AND GUIDELINES

1. VACATION POLICY
All full-time employees are entitled to 15 days of paid vacation per year. Part-time employees receive pro-rated vacation days based on their working hours. Vacation requests must be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance through the HR portal. Unused vacation days can be carried over to the next year, up to a maximum of 5 days.

2. SICK LEAVE POLICY
Employees receive 10 days of paid sick leave per year. Sick leave can be used for personal illness, medical appointments, or to care for immediate family members. Sick leave does not carry over to the next year. For absences longer than 3 consecutive days, a medical certificate is required.

3. WORKING HOURS
Standard working hours are Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, with a one-hour lunch break. Flexible working arrangements are available with manager approval. Remote work is permitted up to 2 days per week for eligible positions.

4. DRESS CODE
Business casual attire is required Monday through Thursday. On Fridays, employees may dress casually, including jeans and sneakers, as long as the attire is appropriate for a professional workplace. Safety equipment must be worn in designated areas such as laboratories and manufacturing floors.

5. EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
Employees can submit expense reports for business-related costs including travel, meals, and supplies. All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date with valid receipts. Reimbursements are processed within 2 weeks of approval. Use the online expense portal to submit claims.

6. PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
The company supports employee growth through annual professional development budgets. Each employee is eligible for up to $2,000 per year for courses, certifications, and conferences related to their role. Prior manager approval is required.

7. PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
Performance reviews are conducted annually in December. Employees receive feedback on their achievements, areas for improvement, and goal setting for the next year. Mid-year check-ins occur in June to track progress.

8. CODE OF CONDUCT
All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct. This includes respect for colleagues, confidentiality of company information, and adherence to all company policies. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
