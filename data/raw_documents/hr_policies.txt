SECTION 1: RECRUITMENT & HIRING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 Job Posting Requirements
- All positions must be posted internally for minimum 5 business days
- External posting requires HR approval
- Job descriptions must include essential functions and qualifications
- Salary ranges must be included in all postings (pay transparency)

1.2 Interview Process
- Minimum 2 rounds of interviews for all positions
- Panel interviews required for manager-level and above
- Skills assessments required for technical roles
- Reference checks mandatory before offer

1.3 Background Checks
- Criminal background check required for all new hires
- Credit check only for finance-related positions
- Education and employment verification standard
- Results reviewed by HR, not hiring manager

1.4 Offer Letters
- Standard offer letter template must be used
- All offers must be approved by HR and Finance
- Verbal offers are not official - written offer required
- Candidates have 5 business days to respond

SECTION 2: ONBOARDING PROCESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 First Day Checklist
□ Complete I-9 verification
□ Sign employee handbook acknowledgment
□ Set up direct deposit
□ Receive company laptop and equipment
□ Complete IT security training
□ Meet with HR for benefits enrollment
□ Tour of office facilities
□ Introduction to team members

2.2 First Week
- Complete all required compliance training
- Set up all software and system access
- Review department goals and expectations
- Schedule 1:1 with manager
- Complete new hire survey

2.3 First 90 Days
- Weekly check-ins with manager
- 30-day informal review
- 60-day progress assessment
- 90-day formal probationary review
- Probation may be extended if needed (up to 180 days)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3: EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 General Guidelines
- Expenses must be reasonable and business-related
- Submit within 30 days of expense
- Original receipts required for expenses over $25
- Manager approval required for all expenses
- Expenses over $500 require VP approval

3.2 Travel Expenses
- Air travel: Coach class required (business class for flights 6+ hours)
- Hotels: Up to $200/night domestic, $300/night international
- Meals: $75/day domestic, $100/day international
- Ground transportation: Economy car rentals, ride-share preferred
- Personal car mileage: $0.67 per mile (IRS standard rate)

3.3 Processing Timeline
- Submit via expense management system
- Manager approval within 3 business days
- Reimbursement via direct deposit within 5 business days after approval
- Disputes should be raised within 10 days

3.4 Non-Reimbursable Items
- Personal entertainment
- Mini-bar charges
- Spouse/companion travel
- Traffic fines or parking tickets
- Personal items lost during travel
- First class upgrades (unless medically required)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4: LEAVE MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 Requesting Time Off
- All leave requests through HR portal
- Vacation: 2 weeks advance notice required
- Sick leave: Notify manager within 30 minutes of start time
- Personal days: 24 hours notice preferred
- FMLA leave: 30 days notice when foreseeable

4.2 Leave Without Pay (LWOP)
- May be granted for extended personal matters
- Maximum 30 days per calendar year
- Benefits continue during LWOP (employee pays full premium)
- Manager and HR approval required
- Not to be used when paid leave is available

4.3 Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
- Eligible after 12 months of employment
- Must have worked 1,250 hours in previous 12 months
- Up to 12 weeks unpaid leave per year
- Job protection guaranteed
- Health benefits continue during leave

4.4 Jury Duty
- Full pay for jury duty (no limit)
- Provide summons to HR
- Expected to work when not actively serving
- Jury compensation may be kept by employee

SECTION 5: WORKPLACE CONDUCT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Anti-Harassment Policy
TechCorp maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment of any kind.

Prohibited behaviors include:
- Verbal harassment (slurs, jokes, comments)
- Physical harassment (unwanted touching, blocking movement)
- Visual harassment (offensive images, gestures)
- Sexual harassment (unwanted advances, requests for favors)
- Cyberbullying (online harassment via any platform)

Reporting:
- Report to manager, HR, or anonymous hotline
- All reports investigated within 48 hours
- Confidentiality maintained to extent possible
- No retaliation for good-faith reporting
- Substantiated claims result in disciplinary action up to termination

5.2 Conflict Resolution
Step 1: Direct conversation with involved party
Step 2: Manager mediation
Step 3: HR involvement
Step 4: Formal grievance process
Step 5: External mediation (if needed)

5.3 Disciplinary Process
Level 1: Verbal warning (documented)
Level 2: Written warning
Level 3: Final written warning
Level 4: Suspension without pay
Level 5: Termination

Note: Serious violations may skip steps and result in immediate termination.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: TERMINATION PROCEDURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Voluntary Resignation
- Two weeks notice requested (4 weeks for managers)
- Exit interview with HR
- Return all company property
- Final paycheck includes unused vacation
- Benefits continue through end of month

6.2 Involuntary Termination
- Documentation required for all terminations
- HR and Legal review required
- Termination meeting with HR present
- Severance based on tenure (if applicable)
- COBRA benefits information provided

6.3 Exit Procedures
□ Return laptop and equipment
□ Return ID badge and keys
□ Complete knowledge transfer
□ Update system access removal
□ Forward email auto-reply setup
□ Provide forwarding address for tax documents


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7: BENEFITS ADMINISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 Health Insurance
- Multiple plan options (PPO, HMO, HDHP)
- Company pays 80% of employee premium
- Company pays 50% of dependent premiums
- Open enrollment: November 1-15 annually
- Life changes allow mid-year enrollment

7.2 Retirement Benefits
- 401(k) plan available after 30 days
- Company match: 100% up to 4% of salary
- Immediate vesting for employee contributions
- Company match vests over 4 years (25% per year)
- Roth 401(k) option available

7.3 Other Benefits
- Life insurance: 2x annual salary (company paid)
- Short-term disability: 60% of salary for up to 12 weeks
- Long-term disability: 60% of salary (after 12 weeks)
- Vision insurance: $150 annual benefit
- Dental insurance: Preventive care 100% covered
- FSA and HSA options available
- Commuter benefits: Up to $300/month pre-tax

7.4 Professional Development
- $2,500 annual learning budget per employee
- Conference attendance (upon manager approval)
- Tuition reimbursement: Up to $10,000/year for approved programs
- Internal training sessions monthly
- Mentorship program available
